The Science Behind
CreditGuard

A transparent look at how the model works, what data it uses, and the statistical factors that determine credit default risk.

What We Do

CreditGuard is a credit default prediction tool that estimates the probability that a credit card holder will fail to pay their next month's bill. It is built on a logistic regression model trained on the publicly available UCI Machine Learning Repository's Default of Credit Card Clients dataset — a benchmark dataset used extensively in financial risk research.

The tool is designed for educational and demonstration purposes, showing how machine learning can be applied to real-world credit risk scenarios. It gives analysts, students, and fintech builders an interactive way to understand the relationship between financial behavior and default risk.

Methodology
Algorithm

Logistic Regression

A classical binary classification algorithm ideal for default prediction. It outputs a probability between 0 and 1 via the sigmoid function, making scores interpretable by non-technical stakeholders.

Dataset

UCI CC Default Dataset

30,000 real credit card account records from a major Taiwanese bank, covering 6 months of billing and payment history per client. A widely-cited benchmark in credit scoring research.

Calibration

Coefficient Weighting

Model coefficients are calibrated to match the statistical relationships found in peer-reviewed analysis of the dataset, with payment status and utilization carrying the heaviest weights.

Output

Probability + Risk Tier

Each prediction produces a percentage probability and a categorical tier (Low / Medium / High), with the four most influential factors highlighted for that specific prediction.

Limitations & Disclaimer

This tool is built for educational and demonstration purposes only and should not be used to make real lending or credit decisions. The model is calibrated on historical data from Taiwan in 2005 and may not generalize to other geographies, time periods, or credit products.

Real-world credit scoring systems use far more features, proprietary data, extensive validation, and are subject to strict regulatory requirements including fair lending laws (e.g., ECOA, FCRA in the US). CreditGuard does not replace a properly validated, audited credit scoring model.
